Venus

Similar in structure and size to Earth, Venus's thick atmosphere traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet in our solar system.

Neptune

Neptune—the eighth and most distant major planet orbiting our Sun—is dark, cold and whipped by supersonic winds. It was the first planet located through mathematical calculations..

Earth

The earth has Life, atmosphere, magnetic field, rotation, water and oceans, located third from Sun. Earth, our cherished home in the cosmos, teems with life, adorned by captivating landscapes, and harbors boundless mysteries.

Saturn

Saturn is a captivating gas giant, the sixth planet from the Sun in our solar system. Saturn's claim to fame is its magnificent ring system, consisting of countless icy and rocky particles that encircle the planet, creating a breathtaking celestial spectacle. This colossal planet is the second-largest in our solar system, with a diameter of about 116,464 kilometers (72,367 miles).

Mercery

From the surface of Mercury, the Sun would appear more than three times as large as it does when viewed from Earth, and the sunlight would be as much as 11 times brighter.

Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet in our solar system. It was once considered the ninth planet, but its classification was changed in 2006 by the International Astronomical Union (IAU) due to its small size and its location in the Kuiper Belt, a region of the solar system beyond Neptune that contains many small icy objects.
